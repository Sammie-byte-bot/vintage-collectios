Our Story

From Nairobi's markets to your collection

TreasureWave Collections started as a weekend habit — digging through Gikomba and Toi Market for the one jacket, the one mug, the one chair that still had decades of life left in it. What began as a personal hobby quickly turned into something bigger when friends kept asking, "where did you find that?"

Today, our small team of buyers and curators travels across Kenya every week, sourcing fashion, electronics, and home pieces that were built to last but somehow got left behind. Each item is inspected, cleaned, and — where needed — repaired before it earns a spot in our collection.

We believe the best things aren't always new. They're well-made, well-loved, and ready for whoever discovers them next.

— The TreasureWave Collections Team
What We Stand For

Three things that don't change

Trends move fast. Our standards don't. Every piece that reaches you has been through the same care, no matter the price tag.

✓

Hand-checked quality

Every item is inspected for condition, cleaned, and tested (where applicable) before it's listed. If it's not good enough for our own collections, it doesn't make the cut.

♻

Slower, kinder collecting

Buying pre-loved means fewer things end up in landfill and fewer new resources are used. Good taste doesn't have to come at the planet's expense.

🇰🇪

Proudly Kenyan-sourced

From the markets of Nairobi to towns across the country, everything we offer is found, sorted, and curated right here in Kenya — supporting local traders along the way.

Where It Comes From

Sourced from the heart of Kenya's markets

Our buying team spends most weeks on the road — through Gikomba, Toi, and smaller town markets — looking for pieces with good bones: solid stitching, real leather, hardwood, and brands worth a second look.

Nothing is mass-produced or drop-shipped. If it's in our collection, someone on our team held it, checked it, and decided it was worth bringing home.
